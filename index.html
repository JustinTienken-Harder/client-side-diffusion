Quarter Circle: Blends prompts with equal L2 norm of X and Y, ideal for when your prompt embeddings are orthogonal, otherwise the interpolation may be 20% extra "Cat" + "Dog".
SLERP Curve: Blends prompts by spherical interpolation, preserves magnitude of mixed prompts in embedding space. Because CLIP embeddings of a prompt are normalized (on the unit sphere), the "optimal" point in between also lies on this sphere.
Simplex Line: Blends prompts with x+y=1, i.e. fixed total weight. If prompts are highly parallel, then the SLERP approaches the simplex line.
This selection tool is just a fun way to explore the latent space of SDXS (a pseudo-acronym), a one-step latent diffusion model, through interpolation of the embeddings. Here are some interpretations/facts/etc:
- The 75% to 25% lines are the proportional blendings between Y and X.
- The text embeddings are quite "conical", and so they tend to point in the same direction. This means points near 0 (or negative) are generic no matter the prompts
- Hold your arms straight out and point at a 90 degree angle. Your hands are glued to a bendy hula hoop with the center at your chest.
- Picking two different prompts is like pointing in two random directions.
- The SLERP curve would be your fingertip if you swept your right arm to your left.
- The simplex line is a maximally taught string between your fingertips.
- The quarter circle is the bendy hula hoop's outer arc between your arms.
