This tool explores the latent space of SDXS through embedding interpolation.
- The text embeddings are quite "conical", and so they tend to point in the same direction. This means points near 0 (or negative) are far from the "meat" of the latent space
- The 75% to 25% dashed lines show proportional blendings between Y and X.
- In pivot mode, α controls how strongly the pivot is subtracted from the other prompts before blending.
- When α=1, the pivot is fully subtracted, resulting in the pivot prompt at origin. When α=0, the pivot has no effect.
- Above 1, the pivot is exaggerated, and can make the blended concepts better.
- The vSLERP ellipse shows where unit-circle angles map in the pivot-adjusted space.
The most distinct prompts (the angle between them at 76 degrees) that I could find are "A Shimmering Deep-Space Nebula" and "Brutalist Concrete Architecture".
Even with these very different prompts, the blended images are often quite coherent and interesting, showing that the model has learned to combine concepts in a smooth way.
An ideal pivot point is something that is extremely conformal. Basically, very generic and abstract concepts
"A picture of some food on a plate" is a good pivot because it has strong, general features that are still somewhat neutral. Utilizing X and Y prompts that are similar to the pivot, but orthogonal to each other provides some of the most interesting interpolation.
"A bowl of spaghetti" and "A tray of fries" pair well with the aformentioned pivot.
The 50% blend in the negative X positive Y quadrant is a tray of fries with all the "bowl of spaghetti" removed (or the spaghettiness unrelated to a picture of some food on a plate).
